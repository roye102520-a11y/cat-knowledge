## Causes of Diarrhea in Cats / 猫咪腹泻的原因

**English**  
Understanding *why* diarrhea happens helps you choose the right next step. In many household cats, the cause is multifactorial: a little stress plus a little diet change can tip the gut over the edge.

**Diet-related causes:** Sudden food changes are a classic trigger. Cats have sensitive digestive systems compared with many dogs. Switching kibble overnight, offering a new treat brand, or letting your cat finish human leftovers can all disrupt the normal balance of bacteria in the gut and lead to soft stool or diarrhea. **Gradual transitions** (often called a seven-day switch) exist for this reason. Food intolerance or allergy can also present as chronic or recurring loose stool. Ingredients are debated endlessly online, but the pattern matters: recurring symptoms despite parasite control and stable environment should push you toward a veterinary diet trial or guided elimination plan—not endless guesswork with random “sensitive stomach” bags.

**Parasites and infections:** Intestinal parasites (for example, roundworms, giardia, or coccidia—exact diagnosis requires fecal testing) are common in cats that go outdoors, hunt, or live in multi-cat environments. Infectious causes, including bacterial overgrowth and viral illness, can produce diarrhea with or without vomiting. Kittens are especially vulnerable because they dehydrate quickly.

**Stress and environment:** Moving furniture, introducing a new pet, loud renovations, or even changing the location of the litter box can create stress-related gut upset. Stress does not explain every case, but it is worth listing because it is under-discussed in SEO articles that only focus on food.

**Toxins and foreign material:** Cats chew plants, strings, and small objects. Some toxins and foreign bodies cause diarrhea; others cause constipation or vomiting first. Any suspicion of toxin ingestion is an emergency topic—this article cannot list every toxic plant, but your vet or a pet poison hotline can.

**Underlying disease:** Pancreatitis, inflammatory bowel disease, hyperthyroidism, and other chronic conditions may include diarrhea among their signs. Persistent diarrhea, weight loss, or changes in appetite should not be “managed” indefinitely with home remedies alone.

**中文**  
弄清**为什么**会腹泻，有助于选择下一步。家猫常见**多因素叠加**：一点压力 + 一点换粮，就可能让肠道失衡。

**与饮食相关：** 突然换粮是经典诱因。猫的消化系统相对敏感，一夜换粮、新零食、人食剩饭都可能打乱肠道菌群，导致软便或腹泻，因此需要**渐进换粮**（常称 7 天换粮法）。食物不耐受或过敏也可表现为反复软便。网上配料争论很多，但**模式**更重要：在驱虫与环境稳定后仍反复软便，应走向兽医指导下的饲粮试验或排除计划，而不是盲目换「肠胃敏感」粮。

**寄生虫与感染：** 肠道寄生虫（如蛔虫、贾第虫、球虫等——确诊需粪便检查）常见于外出、捕猎或多猫环境。细菌过度增殖、病毒感染等也可致腹泻，或伴呕吐。幼猫脱水快，风险更高。

**压力与环境：** 搬家、新宠物、装修噪音、甚至猫砂盆位置改变，都可能引发应激性肠紊乱。压力不能解释所有病例，但不应只谈食物而忽略环境。

**毒物与异物：** 啃咬植物、线头、小物件等可能致病；部分毒物/异物先表现为腹泻，有的先便秘或呕吐。疑似中毒请走急诊通道，本文无法穷举毒物，请咨询兽医或中毒热线。

**基础疾病：** 胰腺炎、炎性肠病、甲亢等慢性病均可出现腹泻。持续腹泻、体重下降或食欲变化，不应长期只靠「家庭偏方」硬扛。

## Solutions: What to Do Step by Step / 对策：分步怎么做

**English**  
Think in layers: **observe**, **support**, **escalate** when red flags appear.

**Step 1:** Note stool consistency, color, frequency, mucus/blood; track appetite, energy, vomiting. A short timeline helps your vet.

**Step 2:** If you recently changed food, consider reverting temporarily and then a slower transition. Pause new treats. For mild cases, small frequent meals of a highly digestible diet—exact choice and duration—should be confirmed with your veterinarian, especially for kittens.

**Step 3:** Hydration first. Diarrhea loses water and electrolytes. Fresh water in multiple locations; some cats drink more from **pet water fountains**.

**Step 4:** Discuss probiotics and supportive care with your vet. Probiotics may help after mild gut upset but are not a universal fix for infections or parasites.

**Step 5:** If diarrhea persists beyond a short window, or if your cat goes outside, fecal testing is a standard next step. Do not repeat random dewormer “roulette” from forums.

**Step 6:** Young cats, senior cats, and cats with other illnesses can deteriorate fast. When in doubt, same-day veterinary care is safer than waiting for a “perfect” home fix.

**中文**  
分层思考：**观察 → 支持 → 出现红旗则升级**。

**第一步：** 记录便形、颜色、频次、黏液/血便；记录食欲、精神、呕吐。简短时间线有助于就诊。

**第二步：** 若刚换粮，可先暂回旧粮再缓慢过渡；暂停新零食。轻症可小量多餐、易消化饲粮——**具体品种与时长须兽医确认**，幼猫尤甚。

**第三步：** 优先补水。腹泻丢失水分与电解质。多放水点；部分猫更爱**循环饮水机**。

**第四步：** 与兽医讨论益生菌及支持治疗。益生菌或对轻度肠道紊乱有帮助，但不能替代驱虫与抗感染等针对性治疗。

**第五步：** 腹泻超过窗口期仍持续，或猫外出，**粪便检查**是常规下一步。勿按论坛随机驱虫。

**第六步：** 幼猫、老年猫、合并症猫恶化快。拿不准时，**当日就医**比等待「完美」偏方更安全。

## Recommended Products / 推荐用品（知识库常见方向）

**English**  
Use these as shopping categories, not automatic prescriptions.

- **Digestive support:** pet probiotics (vet-directed; cat-specific), highly digestible or veterinary diets when diarrhea is recurrent.
- **Hydration:** circulating fountains—pick easy-to-clean models.
- **Litter:** dust-controlled litter; **tofu litters** (e.g. **pidan**, **petshy** appear in many guides) and **mineral litters** (e.g. **网易**, **妮可露**); **mixed litters** are popular for odor and scooping feel.

Your database may also list **兽用益生菌** and **泌尿护理粮**—urinary diets are not for every diarrhea case, but hydration and urinary health are related long-term themes.

**中文**  
以下为**方向性**参考，非自动处方。

- **消化支持：** 兽用益生菌（遵医嘱、选猫用）、反复腹泻时易消化或兽医处方饲粮。
- **饮水：** 循环饮水机，优先选易拆洗、易保持清洁的款式。
- **猫砂：** 控尘、结团稳定；**豆腐砂**（如 **pidan**、**petshy**）、**矿砂**（如 **网易**、**妮可露**）、**混合砂** 在社区指南中常出现。

库内也可能有 **泌尿护理粮** 条目——**并非每例腹泻都适用**；但饮水与长期泌尿健康常被一并讨论。

## Practical Tips / 实用提示

**English**  
1. One change at a time (food, litter, routine).  
2. Track weight—small cats lose a few percent fast.  
3. Scoop more often during episodes; wash the box regularly if parasites are possible.  
4. Avoid human meds and random antibiotics from the internet.  
5. Multi-cat homes: isolate feeding or add boxes to reduce shared parasite stress cycles.

**中文**  
1. 一次只改一项（粮、砂、作息），否则无法判断好坏。  
2. 关注体重，小猫掉重几成比例就可能很危险。  
3. 腹泻期勤铲屎、定期洗盆；怀疑寄生虫时更要注意卫生。  
4. 勿用人药、勿按网帖自行用抗生素。  
5. 多猫家庭可考虑分食、加砂盆，减少共享寄生虫与应激循环。
